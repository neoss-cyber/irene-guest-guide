Hit the beach

Highlights close to Acharavi and a short drive away.

Acharavi Beach

Long, organised beach right in front of the village — cosmopolitan in the centre, quieter at the ends. Views to Erikousa. Sunbeds, watersports, tavernas behind.

Almyros Beach

Long sandy beach next to Acharavi. It isn’t organised — no sunbeds or facilities — so it’s ideal if you prefer a more natural, quiet stretch of sand. Bring your own shade and supplies.

Roda

Next village west of Acharavi. Sandy beach, shallow water, very family-friendly. More bars and restaurants along the front.

Canal d’Amour (Sidari)

Famous rock formations and narrow channel. Unique scenery and a popular photo spot. Sandy coves and organised beach.

Hidden gemWalk

Porto Timoni

Two stunning bays (double beach) near Afionas. A short walk down from the village — one of Corfu’s most beautiful spots. Less organised; take water and shoes for the path.

QuieterDiving

Kerassia

Quieter beach east along the north coast. Good for diving and a relaxed day by the sea.

HarbourLively

Kassiopi

Charming harbour village on the northeast coast with a Byzantine castle, pebble and sand beaches, and plenty of tavernas and bars. Popular and lively in season.

RelaxedQuieter

Agios Spiridon

Small resort and beach area on the north coast. Relaxed atmosphere, clear waters, and easy access. Good for a quiet day by the sea.

Must-try local treats

  • Koum-kuat (kumquat) – Corfu’s signature citrus fruit, used to make liqueur, marmalades, candied fruit and sweets.
  • Νούμπουλο (noumboulo) – A traditional Corfiot cured pork fillet, marinated in wine and spices, then smoked — excellent as a meze or gift.
  • Corfiot beer – Fresh, artisanal beers produced locally (several microbreweries on the island).
  • Μανδολάτο (mandolato) – Soft nougat with almonds and honey.
  • Mandoles – Candied or caramelised almonds.
  • Loukoumi (lokum) – Greek delight, often locally flavoured (kumquat or citrus).
  • Corfiot ginger beer (Tsitstibira) – A refreshing, traditional non-alcoholic drink made with ginger and lemon.

directions_bus
Buses: Green buses (KTEL) run to/from Corfu Town. Official timetable (KTEL Corfu) · Or ask reception.
two_wheeler
Car / scooter: We can arrange rentals for you — ask at reception. Ideal for exploring the north and the rest of the island.
local_pharmacy
Pharmacies & ATM: In Acharavi centre.
emergency
Emergency: European emergency number 112. For medical help, ask reception or at the pharmacy.
water_drop
Water: Tap water is generally not for drinking; use bottled water.
route
Roads: Be careful on the roads — many lanes are narrow, and scooters and bikes are common. Drive on the right.
bug_report
Mosquitoes: They can be around, especially in the evening. Repellent and plug-in deterrents help; you can buy them at the pharmacy or supermarket.
bathroom
Toilets: In all places in Greece you don’t flush toilet paper — use the bin next to the toilet. This is normal and helps avoid blockages.
payments
Cash: Many tavernas and small shops prefer or only take cash. There’s an ATM in Acharavi centre.
translate
Few Greek words: Kalimera (good morning), Efharisto (thank you), Parakalo (please / you’re welcome). Greeks appreciate the effort.
